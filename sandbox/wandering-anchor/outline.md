# The Wandering Anchor — Chapter Outline

## Characters
- **Roger Merryweather** — Former property developer turned protocol designer. Built W-Anchor. Explains everything through mechanical metaphors.
- **Dr. Hannah Shandy** — Mathematical economist. Creator of the FIAR-Index. Lives on a narrowboat. Annotates everything.
- **Father Colin Brown** — Retired forensic accountant, exiled from Scotland Yard. Runs a bookshop in Hay-on-Wye. Waiting for one last case.
- **Monica Candida** — CEO of DoughForge. Compassionate capitalist. Genuinely believes she's helping, which makes her dangerous.
- **Xander Kane** — CTO of GlobeNet. Built "Kubla Khan," an algorithm that completes unfinished works. Loves model railways.
- **Tobias "Tobin" Gradwell** — Roger's former mentor. Venture capitalist. Keeps first editions he's never read.
- **Werner Cipher** — Mathematician in a Scottish lighthouse. Communicates only by handwritten letter.
- **Candida Sterling** — Monica's estranged daughter. Graphic designer. Roger's reluctant assistant.

---

## Act I: The Redirect

**Ch 1: "Ever Had a Wankel?"**
Roger discovers his W-Anchor domain redirects to a site of rotating mechanical parts — a juvenile-seeming attack that is actually the first move in a credibility war. His journal entry that evening opens the book.

**Ch 2: "The Globe"**
Hannah Shandy finds her FIAR-Index paper plagiarised by GlobeNet with the proofs reversed, and approaches Roger on her narrowboat. Two outsiders recognise each other.

**Ch 3: "Monica and the GK Spot"**
The trail leads to DoughForge's "Generate & Kill" strategy — fund creators just enough to establish IP, then bankrupt them. Father Brown enters when he recognises the financial pattern from his Scotland Yard days.

**Ch 4: "Candida, Tobin, and the Colour of the Thing Itself"**
Candida Sterling reveals her mother's connection to Tobin Gradwell and the "gradient potential" system that incrementally alters works. Roger learns his mentor funded the theft.

## Act II: The Pattern

**Ch 5: "The Xanadu Problem"**
Xander Kane's algorithm "Kubla Khan" can complete unfinished works better than their authors — the most disturbing evidence yet, because the forgeries are improvements.

**Ch 6: "The Parliament Responds"**
Roger's networked AI collective identifies Werner Cipher's cryptographic signature embedded in altered works without his knowledge. A build log contains a line of Shelley.

**Ch 7: "The Lighthouse Letters"**
Roger writes to Werner Cipher; the reply arrives three weeks later by second-class post, containing a mathematical proof that the protocol was forked without authorisation.

**Ch 8: "The Swedish Cabin"**
Roger retreats to Sweden to bake bread and write code. The quiet chapter — solitude as resistance, building as defiance, sourdough as metaphor for patience.

## Act III: The Publication

**Ch 9: "The Ration Is Still Arriving"**
The anagram breaks: "ration" = "Torina," a secret financial instrument. Hannah's FIAR-Index, correctly applied, maps the entire extraction network.

**Ch 10: "The Helpful Vandal"**
The institutions fix, deny, and redirect — restoring Roger's domain before the complaint is filed, denying after the fix. The system working as designed, against those it claims to serve.

**Ch 11: "The Build That Worked"**
Candida formats the evidence file and Roger publishes everything via W-Anchor — the protocol they tried to destroy becomes the instrument of their exposure.

**Ch 12: "The Verdict"**
Father Brown's closing notes. Nobody goes to prison. Monica is replaced by someone with a different name and the same MBA. But the record exists, and the next Roger can find it.
