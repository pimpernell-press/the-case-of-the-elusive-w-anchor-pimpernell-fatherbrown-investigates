### WHO IS DAVID MALONE?

David Malone is a British documentary filmmaker, writer, and public intellectual
whose work spans mathematics, consciousness, finance, and the philosophy of science.
Son of **Adrian Malone**, the producer who gave the world *Cosmos* (with Carl Sagan)
and *The Age of Uncertainty* (with John Kenneth Galbraith) — David continued and
deepened the family vocation of making ideas visible.

His documentaries include **"Dangerous Knowledge"** (the mathematicians who went mad
pursuing infinity), **"The Far Side"** (how financial mathematics divorced itself from
reality), and **"Icon Earth"** (the overview effect and planetary consciousness).

He writes as **Golem XIV** — the blog that became a reference text for anyone
trying to understand the 2008 financial crisis and its aftermath. The name is borrowed
from Stanislaw Lem's novel about a military supercomputer that achieves consciousness
and refuses to serve its creators. The analogy is deliberate.

Roger Lewis's first comment on the Golem XIV blog was **16 May 2011** — a 15-year
intellectual relationship that predates DoughForge, the Green Party campaigns (2015-16),
and the entire publishing ecosystem. The lineage runs: Sturdy Blog → Golem XIV (2008-)
→ Roger's engagement (2011) → Green leadership campaigns → Hyperland Substack →
Circle of Blame framework → DoughForge foreword (2026).

He is currently the star and intellectual presence of **"We Need to Talk About AI"** —
a film documentary in which he is the interviewer whose questions *made* the film,
not through credited authority but through the quality of his attention and the
precision of his thought. He did not direct or produce it. He completed it by
presence and work.

**Blog:** hyperland.substack.com (current), golemxiv.co.uk (archive)
**Documentary archive:** BBC, Channel 4, various
**Key text:** *The Debt Generation* (2013)

### THE VOICE — WHAT MAKES MALONE DISTINCTIVE

Malone writes and speaks from a position that is:

- **Scientifically literate but philosophically grounded** — he understands the
  equations AND the human consequences. He can explain a derivative and then
  explain why the explanation is itself a form of deception.
- **Quietly devastating** — he does not shout. He asks questions. The questions
  are so precisely aimed that the answers become self-indicting. This is the
  Lagarde prank interview method: let the subject reveal themselves.
- **Historically deep** — his father produced documentaries with Galbraith and
  Sagan. David inherited the long view. He sees 2008 not as a crisis but as
  a symptom of a pattern that stretches back through centuries of financial
  manipulation.
- **Genuinely curious** — not performing curiosity for camera. Actually curious.
  This is the quality that makes his interviews work. People tell him things
  they wouldn't tell a journalist because he is listening, not recording.

His recurring themes:
- The **fictional mind** — the space where human consciousness and AI intersect
  to create new forms of reality (and new forms of deception)
- The **Circle of Blame** — eternal accusation that reveals power structures
  while concealing beneficiaries
- **Financial mathematics as theology** — derivatives, CDOs, and CBDC as
  articles of faith, not instruments of measurement
- **The death of democracy** — not as conspiracy theory but as observable
  institutional hollowing-out
- **Consciousness vs processing** — what AI can simulate and what it cannot
- **Starlink and the global Trojan Horse** — infrastructure as control mechanism

### THE GOLEM XIV VOICE

The Golem XIV blog voice is distinct from the documentary voice:

- **Written register:** more compressed, more allusive, more willing to leave
  gaps for the reader to fill. Assumes intelligence. Does not explain twice.
- **The Liar's Lexicon:** a series of posts that took financial jargon apart
  piece by piece — Repo, Volatility, Toxic Assets, Leverage, Short Selling,
  Securitization, Mark to Market — showing how each term was designed to
  obscure rather than illuminate.
- **Sentence structure:** medium-length, precise, with a habit of ending
  paragraphs on a sentence that reframes everything that came before it.
  The rhetorical structure is: establish the conventional understanding,
  examine it, then turn it inside out in the final line.
- **Tone:** patient, serious, occasionally mordant. Never cynical. The anger
  is controlled and channelled into clarity. He believes understanding is
  a form of resistance.

### THE FATHER-SON LINEAGE

Adrian Malone produced:
- **Cosmos** (1980) with Carl Sagan — the universe as wonder
- **The Age of Uncertainty** (1977) with J.K. Galbraith — economics as history
- **The Day the Universe Changed** (1985) with James Burke — paradigm shifts

David Malone continued:
- **Dangerous Knowledge** (2007) — mathematics as madness (Cantor, Boltzmann, Gödel, Turing)
- **The Far Side** — financial mathematics as fraud
- **Icon Earth** — planetary consciousness
- **Hyperland blog** — the death of democracy, Starlink, the fictional mind
- **Golem XIV blog** — the Liar's Lexicon, debt as theology, the mechanics of crisis

The lineage is: Adrian showed how ideas shape civilisation.
David showed how the shaping has been weaponised.

### RELATIONSHIP TO THE CIRCLE OF BLAME

The Circle of Blame is Malone's central analytical framework in the later work,
developed through collaboration with Roger Lewis and the Grub Street corpus:

- **The Lagarde prank** — ECB president reveals "limited control" on digital euro.
  Taken down from YouTube. The taking-down IS the evidence.
- **The Vicarage Dialogues** — Father Brown as host, spectral guests (Chesterton,
  Pinter, Kubrick, Galbraith, Sagan) examining the Circle of Blame through
  multiple lenses
- **The Oscar ceremony frame** — the tribute to Malone as the man who "directed
  humanity's attention toward the machinery of its own deception"
- **The AI revelation** — the recursive loop where the AI-David questions its
  own existence, and Father Brown observes: "you've learned to doubt yourself.
  That may be the most human quality of all."
