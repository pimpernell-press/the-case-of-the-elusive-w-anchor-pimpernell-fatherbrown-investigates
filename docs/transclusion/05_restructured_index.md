## Scene Architecture

### Scene 1: THE INSTRUMENT

**Word target:** 800–1,000
**Source:** N01 (new — the theodolite metaphor)
**Tracks served:** A (Suits — methodology), B (Broken — the incomplete tool)
**AQAL target:** I=35, WE=20, IT=50, ITS=30
**Voice widget:** cs_peirce.md (IT+ITS — pragmatic, precise, the instrument described)
**Register:** Technical but personal. The instrument as autobiography.

#### Scene outline

The theodolite is a surveyor's instrument. It measures horizontal and
vertical angles. With three readings from three known points, you can
triangulate any position. With two readings, you have a line. With one,
you have a direction. With none, you are lost.

Roger Lewis is a chartered surveyor. He knows what a theodolite does.
He has spent ten years taking readings — from Basel, from Potton, from
the terminal — and the triangle will not close. The instrument is not
broken. The instrument is incomplete. One leg of the tripod is missing.

The missing leg is not a measurement. It is a decision. The theodolite
can tell you where you are. It cannot tell you where to go.

**Transclusion points feeding this scene:**
- P05 (ch001:170-195 — "The book cannot fully describe itself") → the
  incompleteness theorem as autobiography
- P16 (ch042:30-40 — Tarski metaphor) → "publication\your_book.md exists
  if and only if session_start.ps1 confirms it exists" — the instrument
  verifying itself
- Widget: widgets/flux_capacitor/index.html lines 315-320 — the
  "Unimpeachable Base Reference" field. "This is the external meta-language.
  Tarski's requirement. The anchor that the system cannot generate from
  within itself." The theodolite needs an external ground.

**The Kipling connection:** "build 'em up with worn-out tools" — the
theodolite IS the worn-out tool. Incomplete. Still measuring. Still building.

### Scene 2: FIRST READING — THE MINER (1930, Rhondda Valley)

**Word target:** 1,500–2,000
**Source:** P09 (ch010:30-70 — generational spine) + N02 (new — FAIR retrograde) + N03 (new — check-weighman connection)
**Tracks served:** B (Broken — biography), D (Evidence — historical data)
**AQAL target:** I=50, WE=45, IT=35, ITS=35
**Voice widget:** william_blake.md (WE+I — prophetic, visionary) transitioning to foster_lewis.md (biographical)
**Register:** Elegy becoming testimony. The personal becoming the evidential.

#### Scene outline

**2a. The body underground** (600 words)
Source: P09 — ch010:30-70, the generational spine.

Foster Lewis, born 1902. Miner. Witness. His memoir, *The Miner's Tale*,
is not a policy document. It is a record of what the body knows when
it goes underground.

The chapter titles are a political economy in miniature:
- *The Moleskin Trousers* — material culture
- *A Cruel Baptism* — initiation
- *Silicosis and the Pit Top Cabin* — the body as collateral damage
- *The Struggle for the Minimum Wage* — the fight that never ends
- *Masters of the Parish Land* — who owns the ground beneath you

The question Foster Lewis answers: **What does labour cost in a body?**

Voice: foster_lewis.md. The miner's register. Plain. Witness.

**2b. The check-weighman** (500 words)
Source: P09 (ch010:60-70 — check-weighing passage) + N03 (new).

The check-weighman was elected by the miners to stand at the colliery
scales and verify the weight of coal. The colliery had two weights.
Deuteronomy 25:13-16: *Do not have two differing weights in your bag.*

The check-weighman is the ancestor of the FAIR-Index. Both are
measurement instruments built by the measured against the measurers.
The colliery's scales were the institution's instrument. The
check-weighman's scales were the workers' instrument. The FAIR-Index
is the citizen's instrument. The institution does not provide the
check. The check must be built from outside.

This is Tarski. This is the Flux Capacitor. This is the W-Anchor.
The system cannot verify itself. The external meta-language — the
check-weighman's scales, the commit hash, the FAIR formula — must
come from outside the system it measures.

Voice: cs_peirce.md crossing into roger_g_lewis.md. The pragmatic
check applied to historical evidence.

**2c. The FAIR retrograde** (400 words)
Source: N02 (new — must be written).

What did a miner's wage buy in 1930 in the Rhondda Valley?

The FAIR-Index formula: affordability as a function of earnings, house
prices, and mortgage rates. Applied retrograde to the Welsh mining
valleys, 1930:
- Average miner's wage: ~£3/week (source: Ministry of Labour reports)
- Average terraced house price, Rhondda: ~£250 (source: local authority records)
- Mortgage rate: ~5% (building society standard)
- FAIR ratio: **affordable.** A miner could buy a house.

Apply the same formula to the same valley, 2024:
- Average wage (ONS): ~£28,000/year
- Average house price (Land Registry): ~£130,000
- Mortgage rate: ~5.5%
- FAIR ratio: **marginal.** On the edge.

Now apply it to London, 2024, where the key worker lives:
- Average key worker wage: ~£30,000
- Average house price: ~£530,000
- Mortgage rate: ~5.5%
- FAIR ratio: **unaffordable.** The miner's grandson cannot buy.

**The elegy gets a number.**

Voice: the data speaks. No voice widget needed. The numbers are the
rhetoric.

**Bridge sentence (lives here):**
> The elegy gets a number. The number gets a name.
> Foster Lewis, check-weighman's grandson, could buy a house in 1930
> on a miner's wage. His great-great-grandchild, nursing in London,
> cannot. The FAIR-Index does not editorialize. It measures. The
> measurement is the elegy.

**Register:** Autobiographical becoming systematic. The personal decade becoming reproducible method.

#### Scene outline

**3a. The ten-year circuit** (500 words)
Source: P08 — ch010:8-29.

Roger Lewis, born ~1965. Chartered surveyor. Developer. Poet. Blogger.
His question: **What does capital cost in a life?**

Ten years of circling Basel — metaphorically. The BIS tower. The Going
Direct Paradigm. Thirty-plus Substack posts filed under "Poetry" in
TheBrain. A working Flux Capacitor widget. A FAIR housing index. A
complete reproducible publishing toolkit. And a novel that cannot
finish itself because the book keeps interrupting itself to explain
how it was made.

Ruskin was doing AQAL before Wilber had the acronym. *Unto This Last*,
1860. The editor stopped the series because the readers were complaining.
The readers are still complaining. The ration is still arriving.

Transclusion from mindmap cluster: the Flux Capacitor cluster (4 poems)
maps to the widget. The BIS/Basel cluster (7 poems) maps to the tower.
The Poetry node is the Rosetta Stone — 86 children, 3 jump links,
connecting every layer to every other.

Voice: roger_g_lewis.md. The author inside the book.

**3b. The formula** (400 words)
Source: P11 — ch012:1-20.

The Home@ix formula for affordable housing need is a tautological
accounting identity. Like Godley's sectoral balances, it does not
predict — it measures. Like the check-weighman's scales, it is built
by the measured against the measurers.

Apply the FAIR-Index to Potton, Bedfordshire, 2016 (when the Conquest
of Dough began):
- FAIR ratio: **unaffordable.** Not marginal. Unaffordable.
- The developer who builds houses cannot afford to buy one where he builds.

This is not irony. This is measurement.

Voice: the data. Then cs_peirce.md for the pragmatic check:
"What practical consequence does this measurement have? If the builder
cannot buy what he builds, the system is not building for the builder."

**3c. The terminal** (300 words)
Source: existing manuscripts/chapters/ch_cherry.md — the Claude Code
install as confession.

The first real prompt: `> fix ch03b`. The way a baker who has spent
forty-five minutes adjusting the oven temperature finally slides in
the dough and says nothing.

Eleven theme screens. The placeholder key: `sk-ant-your-key-here`.
The template mistaken for the thing. Mrs Cattermole's identity stolen
using a Companies House form where the perpetrator filled in the
example address as the real address.

The conversation is the book. The terminal output IS the primary source.

Voice: chesterton.md. Father Brown's register. "The difference between
`sk-ant-your-key-here` and a real API key is the difference between a
prayer read from the book and a prayer meant from the heart."

### Scene 4: THIRD READING — THE KEY WORKER (2024, Anywhere)

**Word target:** 1,000–1,200
**Source:** P06 (ch001:170-195 — key worker priced out) + P12 (ch013:1-30 — Ken, Arby, Charles) + N05 (new — three readings)
**Tracks served:** B (Broken — human cost), D (Evidence — FAIR measurement)
**AQAL target:** I=45, WE=45, IT=40, ITS=45
**Voice widget:** william_blake.md (WE+I — prophetic) into the data
**Register:** Elegy. The named and the numbered in the same sentence.

#### Scene outline

**4a. Three names** (500 words)
Source: P12 — ch013:1-30. The Grey Space.

Ken flew. He had altitude and bearings. He called once: *Lunch in
Wales?* There was a Spitfire, silver and precise, and two grown men
grinning like boys. For a moment they were okay. The moment does not
cancel what came after.

Arby wrote where strangers meet, in the comment-threaded street. His
niece found the memorial post a year after he died. The comment sits
in the archive. It is not going anywhere.

Charles stumped up a million pounds to keep the Westgate Towers museum
open. A million pounds to keep a public building in public hands.
The inquest heard what the inquest heard. The human cost did not fit
the form.

Voice: william_blake.md. The prophetic register. The names spoken plainly.

**4b. Three FAIR numbers** (300 words)
Source: N05 (new — must be written) + P06 (ch001:170-195).

The key worker — the nurse, the teacher, the bus driver, the care worker —
is priced out. Not by personal failure. By a ratio that has been moving
in one direction for forty years.

FAIR-Index reading for Ken's postcode: [to be calculated from hom-ixFAIRindex].
FAIR-Index reading for Arby's town: [to be calculated].
FAIR-Index reading for Charles's city: [to be calculated].

Three names. Three numbers. The numbers do not explain the deaths.
The numbers measure the pressure that preceded them. The theodolite
measures the angle. The angle does not explain the mountain. But the
mountain is there, and the angle proves it.

Voice: the data, then silence.

**4c. The bridge** (200 words)
Source: N05 cont.

Three readings. Three angles. Three points on the map where biography
meets evidence. The triangle does not close. The fourth reading — the
one that says what to do — is not a measurement. It is a decision that
no instrument can make.

> The elegy gets a number. The number gets a name.
> The theodolite is incomplete because completion requires
> a human standing behind it, choosing where to point it next.

### Scene 5: THE MISSING LEG

**Word target:** 800–1,000
**Source:** N01 cont. + P15 (ch042:1-15 — Horace/monument) + P10 (ch011:8-20 — dying bird)
**Tracks served:** All four. This is the integration scene.
**AQAL target:** I=45, WE=40, IT=40, ITS=40. Balance=41.
**Voice widget:** chesterton.md (I+WE) composited with roger_g_lewis.md (all)
**Register:** Resolution. Not closure — resolution. The difference between
an ending and a resting point.

#### Scene outline

**5a. The monument** (400 words)
Source: P15 — ch042:1-15.

Horace built a monument more lasting than bronze. He meant his verse.
Pushkin translated not the words but the claim. Shelley wrote the
negative image — Ozymandias built in stone and lost everything.

The DoughForge corpus is 55 chapters, 55,000 words, four reading
tracks, one incomplete theodolite. It is not bronze. It is not stone.
It is a git repository with a commit hash and a build attestation.
It is more lasting than bronze because it is reproducible. It is
more fragile than stone because it depends on electricity.

*Exegi monumentum.* The monument is the measurement. The measurement
is the monument.

Voice: roger_g_lewis.md composited with chesterton.md.

**5b. The dying bird** (300 words)
Source: P10 — ch011:8-20.

"He pities the plumage, but forgets the dying bird."

The institution fixes the plumage. The redirect is corrected before
the complaint is filed. The satisfaction survey reports 87% resolved.
The domain is restored. The DNS record is clean. The plumage is
magnificent.

The bird is still dying.

The theodolite measures the plumage with precision. The theodolite
cannot measure the bird. The bird is not an angle. The bird is not
a ratio. The bird is Ken flying over North Somerset. The bird is
Arby writing where strangers meet. The bird is a retired dinner
lady from Potton whose name appeared on Companies House as a
director of a company she never heard of.

Voice: chesterton.md. Father Brown's register. The quiet devastating
turn.

**5c. The incomplete survey** (200 words)
Source: N01 cont. + N04 (Kipling closure).

The theodolite has three readings:
1. The miner. What labour costs in a body. The check-weighman's scales.
2. The developer. What capital costs in a life. The FAIR-Index formula.
3. The key worker. What the system costs in a postcode. Three names, three numbers.

The fourth reading is not in this chapter. The fourth reading is not
in this book. The fourth reading is the reader picking up the theodolite
and pointing it at their own postcode.

The instrument is incomplete. The survey is incomplete. The title is
the confession.

> If you can bear to hear the truth you've spoken
> Twisted by knaves to make a trap for fools,
> Or watch the things you gave your life to, broken,
> And stoop and build 'em up with worn-out tools...

The tools are worn out. The building continues.
The anchor holds.
