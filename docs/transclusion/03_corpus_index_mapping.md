# Stage 03: Corpus Index Mapping — Four Reading Tracks
## One Corpus, Four Doors

**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Source:** `reservoir/ntgsj_index.csv` (headers only — no rows populated)
**Actual index source:** Stage 01 extraction + Stage 02 scoring + mindmap_data.json
**Method:** Each of 55 chapters classified into one or more tracks based on
AQAL profile, content analysis, and voice widget affinity.

## The Four Tracks

This is not four books. It is one corpus with four reading orders.
The same dough, shaped into four loaves for four tables.

### Track A — "The Suits" (Conquest of DoughForge)
**Reader:** Systems thinkers. Policy people. The person who reads McKinsey reports
but suspects they're missing something. The AQAL-literate. The measured.
**AQAL signature:** IT + ITS dominant. Balance > 40. Evidence-based.
**Voice:** Peirce, Cuppy. The footnote register. The proof.
**Entry point:** The FAIR paper. The formula. The protocol.
**Promise:** "Here is how the system works. Here are the numbers."

### Track B — "The Broken Tools" (The Incomplete Theodolite)
**Reader:** The biographical reader. The person who has been broken by an
institution and needs to know someone else was broken too — and kept going.
Kipling's "If—" reader. Despair-to-agency.
**AQAL signature:** I dominant or I+WE. Bias < +10. Inner life present.
**Voice:** Blake, Roger G. Lewis. The prophetic register. The personal.
**Entry point:** The Grey Space. The Dying Bird. The Drift.
**Promise:** "Here is what it cost. Here is why it was worth it."

### Track C — "The Comedy Door" (Novels)
**Reader:** The person who doesn't read policy papers. Picks up a novel.
Laughs. Realises halfway through that the joke is about their mortgage.
Pete & Dud. Wodehouse. Father Brown. Laughter as Trojan horse.
**AQAL signature:** WE dominant or balanced. Comedy register detectable
(dialogue format, Pete & Dud, Shandean digressions).
**Voice:** Wodehouse, Chesterton, Cuppy. The comic register.
**Entry point:** "Ever Had a Wankel?" The Hand Shandy frame. The Scrabble game.
**Promise:** "This is very funny. Also, you're being robbed."

### Track D — "The Evidence" (FAIR-Index)
**Reader:** Campaigners. Journalists. Anyone building a case. The person who
needs a number, a citation, a source they can point to in a meeting.
**AQAL signature:** IT maxed. ITS high. The apparatus.
**Voice:** The data speaks for itself. No rhetoric needed.
**Entry point:** The FAIR paper. The Bibliography. The Transclusion Register.
**Promise:** "Here is the evidence. Use it."

## What Stage 03 Reveals for Stage 04

1. **The Father Brown novella is Track C** (the Comedy Door) but currently
   lacks comedy. It has the detective frame but not the Wodehouse/Pete & Dud
   register. The transclusion map must inject Track C material.

2. **Track B↔D gap is the structural problem.** Biography and evidence
   never share a chapter in the DoughForge corpus. Father Brown is the
   bridge character — he connects personal sin to documented crime.
   The novella must be where B meets D.

3. **ch001 (Hand Shandy) is the master index.** It contains compressed
   versions of all four tracks in a single Shandean chapter. It is
   the map of the whole territory, disguised as a digression.

4. **The 86 TheBrain children are the unindexed reservoir.** The ntgsj_index.csv
   is empty. Populating it — mapping each Substack post to its track(s) —
   would complete the corpus index and enable programmatic track generation.

5. **Each track has its own voice widget palette.** The shade pass for any
   chapter should select voice widgets based on which track the chapter
   primarily serves.
