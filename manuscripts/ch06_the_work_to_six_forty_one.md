# Chapter 6: The Work to Six Forty-One

*In which Father Brown examines a timestamp, Flambeau learns that
the devil is in the metadata, and a very specific minute of a very
specific day tells a story that no one intended to tell.*

---

"Six forty-one," said Father Brown.

He said it the way a priest says a saint's feast day — with quiet
reverence for something most people would walk past without noticing.

"Six forty-one in the morning?" asked Flambeau.

"In the evening. Eighteen forty-one, if you prefer the military
notation, which I do, because this is a kind of war." Father Brown
set down a printout — a system log, obtained through a Subject
Access Request that had taken Roger four months and two formal
complaints to extract. "This is the record of the change. The
moment Roger's domain was redirected. Six forty-one p.m."

Flambeau looked at it. A line of text. A timestamp. An action.

"Why does the time matter?"

"Because," said Father Brown, "six forty-one p.m. is not an
accident time. It is not a server-glitch time. Servers glitch at
three in the morning, during maintenance windows, during batch
processes that run while humans sleep. Six forty-one p.m. is a
*human* time. It is the time a person does something at their desk
before leaving for the day."

He let that settle.

"Someone sat at a keyboard. Someone logged into a system. Someone
made a change to Roger's domain. And then someone went home for
dinner."

---

## The Significance of Timestamps

Father Brown had learned, over many years of hearing confessions,
that people lie about *what* they did but rarely think to lie about
*when* they did it. The timestamp is the confession that the sinner
doesn't know they're making.

"Consider what six forty-one tells us," he said.

1. **It was manual.** Automated processes run on schedules — midnight,
   hourly, on the quarter-hour. 6:41 is not a scheduled time. A human
   pressed a button.

2. **It was end-of-day.** In a UK office, 6:41 p.m. is late. Most
   staff leave by 5:30. Someone was either working late or — more
   likely — doing something they preferred to do when fewer colleagues
   were watching.

3. **It was not logged as a support action.** Roger had no open ticket.
   No request had been made. No authorisation had been given. The change
   was made to a domain whose owner had not asked for anything to be
   changed.

"So someone," said Flambeau, "walked into Roger's house at six
forty-one in the evening, moved all the furniture around, and left
before anyone noticed."

"And when Roger noticed the next morning," said Father Brown, "and
called to complain, he was told that the furniture had always been
that way. Six forty-one. Remember that time, Flambeau. It is the
one honest thing in this entire affair — a timestamp that tells the
truth because nobody thought to lie about it."
