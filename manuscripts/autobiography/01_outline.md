# The Incomplete Theodolite: An Abstract Autobiography
## Dual-Voice Outline — The 40-Year Gap (1965–2005)

**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Title:** *The Incomplete Theodolite: An Abstract Autobiography in Ten Readings*
**Subtitle:** *Being the missing leg of the tripod, the 40 years between the miner and the blogger, narrated in two voices that do not always agree.*
**Word target:** 25,000–30,000 words (10 chapters, 2,500–3,000 each)
**Audiobook target:** 10 chapters at ~15–18 minutes each = 2.5–3 hours total

---

## The Two Voices

**VOICE 1 — THE SURVEYOR (Third Person)**
Register model: the Poetic Legislator biography. "Roger Lewis, MRICS..."
Tone: the external record. Dates, qualifications, properties, transactions.
The CV that anyone could reconstruct from Companies House filings and
RICS membership records. Dispassionate. Factual. The theodolite reading
the angles.

**VOICE 2 — THE WITNESS (First Person)**
Register model: the 245-word foreword. "I remember my sister Suzanne..."
Also: "Notes on an Old Fiddle" (Poem 49). "99 Flakes for Sale" (Poem 50).
"Searching for Racing Cars" (Poem 51).
Tone: the interior record. What was forming underneath. The reading. The
anger. The slow recognition. The family. The moments that don't fit the
CV. Warm, rigorous, angry in service of hope. Roger's own voice when
the prophetic register is set aside and the man speaks plainly.

**The interleave:** Each chapter alternates — a Surveyor paragraph, then
a Witness paragraph, then Surveyor, then Witness. The two voices comment
on the same period from outside and inside. Where they agree, the
reading is complete. Where they disagree, the gap is visible. The
theodolite measures from two stations; the reader triangulates.

### Chapter 1: The Ration (1965–1975)

**Period:** Birth, childhood, the mining household, the brewery ration.
**The Surveyor:** Roger G. Lewis, born approximately 1965, grew up in a household shaped by the mining communities of South Wales. Paternal grandfather Foster Lewis, a miner whose memoir *The Miner's Tale* would later be typed by Roger's sister Suzanne and circulated within the family. Maternal grandfather Llewellyn Harrison.
**The Witness:** I remember long evenings sat at the dining table with Nanny listening to the television and Grampy and Dad chatting away with a glass of Worthington's pale ale, from the ration still sent every month from the brewery. The ration was real. The ration arrived. Nobody questioned it. It was not a metaphor. It was beer.
**Connects forward to:** DoughForge ch008 — "The Ration Still Arriving." The metaphor that began as a brewery delivery.
**Corpus source:** Poetic Legislator foreword (the only existing autobiographical passage for this period).

### Chapter 3: The Instrument (1983–1990)

**Period:** Training. Surveying qualification. Learning to measure.
**The Surveyor:** Roger Lewis qualified as a chartered surveyor (MRICS). The Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors — founded 1868, Royal Charter 1881. A surveyor's training: valuation, measurement, building pathology, land law. The instruments: the theodolite, the chain, the level, the staff. Later: the laser, the GPS, the total station.
**The Witness:** They taught me to measure. That is what a surveyor does. You stand at a known point, you sight through the instrument, you read the angle. If the reading is accurate, the building stands plumb. If the reading is wrong, the wall leans. I did not know then that I would spend twenty years measuring the wrong things accurately.
**Connects forward to:** N01 (Incomplete Theodolite Chapter 1) — the theodolite metaphor. The instrument described in that chapter is the same instrument learned in this one.
**Corpus source:** Home@ix corpus index — "A chartered surveyor by training... over two decades of experience in residential development."

### Chapter 4: The Scales (1990–1997)

**Period:** Early career. Property development. The boom. Learning to read the market's scales.
**The Surveyor:** Roger Lewis entered residential development during the recovery from the 1990–92 recession. Founded RLD Limited, the entity from which Home@ix would later evolve. Residential valuations. Development appraisals. Site acquisition. Planning applications. The mechanics of turning land into houses and houses into mortgages.
**The Witness:** The scales were wrong. I did not know it yet. I was reading the scales the way the colliery read the scales — the company's scales, the scales that always favoured the company. The valuations were accurate. The measurements were precise. The instrument was honest. The question nobody asked was: whose scales are these?
**Connects forward to:** N03 (check-weighman fragment) — "He did not set out to. He set out to build houses. The scales were wrong." This chapter is the full story behind that sentence.

### Chapter 6: 99 Flakes (2001–2005)

**Period:** Fatherhood. The ice cream. The hand. The healing.
**The Surveyor:** Continued property development through the early 2000s. The housing market accelerating. Buy-to-let mortgages introduced 1996, now reshaping the market. The developer watches the market he serves become unrecognisable.
**The Witness:** My son and I had some time in Hoganas, a local seaside town. It brought back memories of my own experience as a child growing up, and parenting I am trying not to repeat. The cone drops. I cry within and remember the pain. With love a cone is restored and he smiles. Let one sad little boy grow to a father. He will grow to a man. Never the twain will visit our lives again. No more pain. Heal the pain.
**Connects forward to:** The Incomplete Theodolite bridge sentence — "The elegy gets a number. The number gets a name." The ice cream has no FAIR number. The ice cream is the reading the theodolite cannot take. It is the missing leg.

### Chapter 7: The Wrong Things Accurately (2005–2008)

**Period:** The recognition. The financial crisis approaching. The surveyor sees the scales.
**The Surveyor:** Property market valuations 2005–2007. Northern Rock. Self-certification mortgages. 125% LTV. The numbers the surveyor is asked to sign. RICS Red Book valuations — "Market Value: the estimated amount for which an asset or liability should exchange on the valuation date." The definition is precise. The market is insane.
**The Witness:** I spent twenty years measuring the wrong things accurately. Every valuation I signed was correct. The comparable evidence supported the figure. The methodology was sound. The Red Book was followed. And the entire edifice was a check-weighman's nightmare — honest scales reading dishonest weights. The coal was real. The weight on the docket was correct. The system that translated the weight into a wage was rigged.
**Connects forward to:** Poetic Legislator Book III — "The Conquest of Dough." The moment the surveyor stops measuring and starts asking who owns the scales. Also: Ruskin's unsigned quantities (DoughForge ch010) — the moral sign the surveyor was trained never to calculate.
**Corpus source:** Book III of Poetic Legislator (the awakening, reframed from third to first person). N02 (FAIR retrograde) — the same formula the surveyor was trained to use, now applied to show what it was always measuring.
**Word target:** 3,000 | **Audio:** ~18 min

### Chapter 8: The Devon Strawberry (2008–2010)

**Period:** The crash. The strawberry fields. The moment of recognition.
**The Surveyor:** 2008 financial crisis. Northern Rock nationalised February 2008. Lehman Brothers September 2008. RLD Limited navigating the collapse. Property valuations in freefall. The instruments still work. The market they measure has changed beneath them.
**The Witness:** The strawberry fields of Devon, capable of producing far more fruit than the market will bear. Artificial scarcity in the midst of plenty. I stood in a field of rotting strawberries and understood, for the first time with my whole body, that the system does not fail. The system succeeds. It succeeds at creating scarcity from abundance, debt from production, hunger from harvest. The strawberries are the proof. The formula is the same formula I was trained to use. The moral sign is negative.
**Connects forward to:** Poetic Legislator Book II — "The Strawberry Conspiracy." Also: the Flux Capacitor widget name — "Flux Capacitor of Strawberry Politics." The strawberry IS the origin of the "Strawberry" in the widget title.

## The Title

**The Incomplete Theodolite: An Abstract Autobiography in Ten Readings**

"Abstract" because:
- The autobiography abstracts from the full life — it covers 40 years in 25,000 words
- The autobiography abstracts the personal into the analytical — each chapter connects to a corpus element
- The autobiography is an abstract in the academic sense — a summary of the longer work (the life) that points the reader to the primary sources (the poems, the blogs, the FAIR-Index, the DoughForge corpus)

"Ten Readings" because:
- A theodolite takes readings
- Ten chapters = ten readings
- Three readings close a triangle, but ten readings map a landscape
- The autobiography is a landscape survey, not a triangulation

## Audiobook Structure

**Format:** 10 chapters at 2,000–3,000 words = 12–18 minutes each.
**Total runtime:** ~2.5–3 hours.
**Design principle:** Each chapter fits a dishwasher cycle (~15 minutes). You put the dishes in, you press play, you listen to one reading. When the cycle ends, the chapter ends. The domestic and the intellectual occupy the same duration. This is not a coincidence. This is the point.

| Ch | Title | Minutes | Voice Ratio (Surveyor:Witness) |
|----|-------|---------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | The Ration | ~15 | 30:70 — mostly Witness (the childhood is personal) |
| 2 | The Moleskin Trousers | ~15 | 40:60 — the landscape needs context |
| 3 | The Instrument | ~18 | 60:40 — the training is external, the recognition internal |
| 4 | The Scales | ~18 | 50:50 — the career and the doubt in equal measure |
| 5 | Notes on an Old Fiddle | ~15 | 20:80 — the family is almost entirely interior |
| 6 | 99 Flakes | ~15 | 20:80 — the fatherhood is interior |
| 7 | The Wrong Things Accurately | ~18 | 50:50 — the recognition bridges inside and outside |
| 8 | The Devon Strawberry | ~15 | 40:60 — the crash is external, the seeing is internal |
| 9 | The First Blog Post | ~15 | 30:70 — the words are the witness arriving in public |
| 10 | The Incomplete Survey | ~12 | 50:50 — resolution. Both voices converge. |

**Audiobook voice notes:** Two voices can be rendered as:
- Two narrators (one male Surveyor, one male Witness — same person, different register)
- Or one narrator with a shift: the Surveyor sections read in a measured, slightly formal register; the Witness sections read in a warmer, closer register. The difference is not accent — it is distance. The Surveyor stands across the room. The Witness sits beside you.
