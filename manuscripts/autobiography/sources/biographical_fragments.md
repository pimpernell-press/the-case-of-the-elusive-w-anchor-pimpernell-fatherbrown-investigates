## Major Discovery: The Barford St Michael Transcript

The single most important find. A video transcript in `[Forge]/All components of book and paths/11 march Claude 4.6 Sonnet.md` (lines 9145-9194) contains spoken autobiographical material covering Chapters 2-5:

> "I spent the formative years of my life in Oxfordshire I went to the Warriner school and Bloxom"
> "I was brought up in a small village called Barford St Michael"
> "as a young man I was the gardener there for Mrs Stapleton"
> "I set myself the task that I wanted to buy that house... what I did do is I bought the Belmont estate"
> "my self-imposed exile here in Sweden"
> "when I left Oxfordshire in my 16th year so this is going back now to 1980... my family moved to West Germany... my father worked for the government"
> "I attended the first Fairport Convention concert... at Broughton Castle"
> "my first wife heard me speak was reading a passage from... Cider with Rosie my five years in Oxfordshire gave me an Oxfordshire accent"
> "Kent School Rhine darlin the military boarding school"
> "I played village cricket for Bloxom"

**This rewrites the Chapter 2 outline.** The childhood was in Oxfordshire (Barford St Michael), not South Wales. The Valleys were the grandparents' world, visited. The formative landscape was English village life — Warriner school, Bloxom cricket, Fairport Convention at Broughton Castle. Then at 16 (~1980), the family moved to West Germany (father in government). Then a military boarding school (Kent School on the Rhine).

## Major Discovery: The Letter to Roy

`[Forge]/All components of book and paths/11 march Claude 4.6 Sonnet.md` (lines 11887-11901) — a first-person letter describing the business career:

> "I was in the property Business I had a Residential and Commercial Estate Agency/consultancy in London Docklands and A group of development companies which I disposed of between 2000 and 2005. I built up my business from 1990 to 2000 and concentrated solely on my development and investment companies from 1997 to 2003. I always kept my teams very small... my intention was never to create some sort of Business Empire my sole objective was very narrow in that I wanted solely to make Money and get out. I really didn't enjoy the experience It made me very selfish and ultimately very ill although Financially it made me independent. My Business career showed me the worse of human greed in myself..."

**This is the Chapter 4 (The Scales) Witness voice.** First-person, confessional, honest about the cost.
