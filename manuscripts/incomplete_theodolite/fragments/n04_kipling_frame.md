# N04: The Kipling Frame

*Voice: Kipling quoted, not imitated. The epigraph and the closing echo.*
*Scenes: Chapter epigraph + 5c closing. ~100 words.*

---

## Epigraph

> If you can bear to hear the truth you've spoken
> Twisted by knaves to make a trap for fools,
> Or watch the things you gave your life to, broken,
> And stoop and build 'em up with worn-out tools...
>
> — Rudyard Kipling, "If—" (1895)

## Closing Echo (Scene 5c)

The theodolite is a worn-out tool. Not because it is old — the mathematics of angular measurement have not changed since Euclid — but because the surveyor has been carrying it for a long time, and the tripod has a missing leg, and the people who benefit from an incomplete survey have no interest in supplying the fourth.

Stoop and build.
