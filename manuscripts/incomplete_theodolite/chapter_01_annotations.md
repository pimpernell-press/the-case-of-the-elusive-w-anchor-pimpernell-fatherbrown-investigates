## Scene 4c: The Bridge (§IV "The Bridge")

- **Track:** B (Broken Tools) + D (Evidence) — **THE JUNCTION**
- **AQAL target:** I=45, WE=35, IT=40, ITS=40
- **Voice:** roger_g_lewis.md
- **Source:** N05 (new)

### Voice notes
- "The elegy gets a number. The number gets a name." — the bridge sentence. Short. Hard. Roger's signature: the long sentences are the evidence, the short sentences are the verdict. This is the verdict.
- "the ratio is the distance between what a life earns and what a roof costs, and that distance has been widening for forty years, and the instrument has been measuring it, and the measurement is the elegy" — classic Roger: the long sentence that spirals inward, accumulating clauses, each load-bearing, arriving at a conclusion that could not have been reached in fewer words.
- Final sentence: "a system that extracts from the living and accounts to nobody" — the Foucauldian pivot (signature move #8). Not "who does it" but "to whom does it account?" The answer (nobody) is the indictment.
