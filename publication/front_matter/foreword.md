This is a story about a small priest who notices things. Not because the things are hidden but because the things are so familiar they have become invisible.

Father Brown does not solve the case. He identifies it. The difference between a surgeon and a diagnostician, between a plumber and a surveyor, between the man who fixes the scales and the man who reads them.

The scales in this story belong to everyone. The weights are public record. The measurement has been running for twenty-seven years. The question is not whether the scales balance. The question is whether anyone is willing to stand at them and say what they show.
